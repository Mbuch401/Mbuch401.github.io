Clucks, Codes, and Coops.

Published on September 24, 2024

Building my poultry monitoring project has been a journey of innovation, determination, and a touch of barnyard intrigue. Armed with an ESP32 and the highly sensitive MAX9814 microphone, I set out to create a sound-based anomaly detection system that could listen to the subtle nuances of my flock's clucks, crows, and coos. Think of it as a tech-savvy chicken whisperer, but instead of soothing words, it uses data to decode the mysteries of poultry chatter.

Of course, the process wasn’t all smooth feathers. I started with the KY-037 microphone but quickly realized it picked up more noise than useful sound, even after tinkering with an LM386 amplifier. Enter the MAX9814, the MVP of this operation, bringing clarity to the chaos. And let’s not forget the trials of working with audio data—like ensuring accurate sampling using the Nyquist-Shannon theorem (yes, even chickens require math).

The fun didn’t stop there. I had to tackle challenges like logging data onto an SD card for easy access and testing the system's sensitivity to different flock sizes. I wanted something adaptable—whether monitoring a small brood of six or a bustling coop of fifty. This wasn’t just a project; it was a solution for real-world issues, like detecting distress in my chickens early enough to intervene.

Every cluck and chirp taught me something new about merging machine learning with embedded systems. From recording and analyzing the poultry orchestra to visualizing patterns with LEDs, this project became my feathered sandbox for testing ideas. And the best part? Knowing that with every beep and buzz of the ESP32, my chickens are safer, and I’m a little closer to revolutionizing poultry care.
